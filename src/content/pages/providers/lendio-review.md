## Key Terms

### APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
APR is the annualized cost of a loan including interest and fees. Through Lendio's network, APR ranges from approximately 10–13% for SBA 7(a) loans to 40–200%+ effective APR for merchant cash advances. Always compare APR — not just monthly payment — across offers.

### Draw Period
For business lines of credit available through Lendio's network, the draw period is the window when you can borrow against your credit limit. Each draw creates its own repayment schedule. Draw periods and terms vary by the specific lender that extends an offer.

### Origination Fee
Many lenders in Lendio's network charge an origination fee of 1–5% of the loan amount, deducted from the funded amount. This fee is included in the APR calculation — confirm it in any offer you receive before accepting.

### Revolving Credit
A revolving line of credit — available through Lendio's LOC (line of credit) matches — lets you draw, repay, and draw again up to your credit limit. Unlike a term loan, you only pay interest on what you've drawn. This is useful for ongoing operating capital needs rather than a one-time purchase.

## Summary Verdict

Lendio is a Utah-based small business loan marketplace that connects businesses to over 75 lenders — including Small Business Administration (SBA) lenders, equipment lending companies, merchant cash advance (MCA) providers, and business line of credit lenders — through a single application. Rather than lending directly, Lendio acts as a matchmaker, submitting your application to multiple lenders and presenting the best offers. This model is valuable for businesses that don't know which product or lender fits their situation. The trade-off is that Lendio earns referral fees from lenders, which may influence which offers are presented. It is a legitimate starting point for businesses exploring multiple financing options, particularly SBA loans.

**Best for:** Business owners who want to compare offers across multiple lenders and product types with one 15-minute application — particularly those exploring SBA loans, equipment financing, or working capital loans simultaneously and unsure which product fits their situation.

**Not ideal for:** Businesses that already know exactly which product and lender they want (apply directly to avoid sharing your data with multiple lenders), or businesses that prefer a direct lender relationship without intermediary referral fees factored into the offer.

## What Lendio Offers

Lendio is a marketplace, not a lender. It collects your business information through one application and submits it to relevant lenders in its network. You receive offers from interested lenders and can compare them before accepting.

**Product types available through Lendio's network:**

**SBA Loans:** Lendio works with SBA Preferred Lenders and can facilitate SBA 7(a) and SBA 504 loans. SBA loans require the most documentation and take 30–60+ days to fund, but carry the lowest rates available to small businesses (typically 10–13% APR). If you qualify for SBA financing, it is almost always the best financial choice.

**Small Business Term Loans:** Fixed-term loans from $5,000 to $2,000,000 through network lenders. Terms, rates, and eligibility vary by lender.

**Business Lines of Credit:** Revolving credit lines from $1,000 to $500,000. Can be drawn and repaid as needed.

**Equipment Financing:** Equipment loans and leases from $5,000 to $5,000,000 through Lendio's equipment financing partners.

**Merchant Cash Advances:** Lendio connects businesses to MCA providers in its network. As with all MCAs, effective APR is high — treat this as a last resort option.

**Invoice Factoring:** Lendio can match businesses to factoring companies in its network.

**SBA Microloans:** For businesses needing less than $50,000, Lendio can help connect to SBA microloan programs.

## How the Marketplace Model Works

Understanding Lendio's business model is important for evaluating its offers:

1. You submit one application with your business and financial information
2. Lendio matches your profile to relevant lenders in its network
3. Interested lenders make offers
4. You compare offers and select one
5. Lendio receives a referral fee from the lender you choose

The referral fee is paid by the lender, not by you directly — but it may be reflected in the rate or terms offered compared to applying directly to the same lender. Lendio is transparent that it operates this way.

**Benefit:** You compare multiple offers simultaneously without applying separately to each lender. This can reveal better options than you'd find through manual research.

**Trade-off:** Your data is shared with multiple lenders. You may receive marketing outreach from lenders in Lendio's network even if you don't accept their offer.

## Cost Transparency

Because Lendio is a marketplace, costs depend entirely on which lender and product you're matched with. Lendio shows you the offers it receives and helps you compare. General cost ranges by product:

| Product | Typical Cost Range |
|---|---|
| SBA 7(a) loan | 10–13% APR |
| Business term loan (online lender) | 15–45% APR |
| Business line of credit | 15–40% APR |
| Equipment financing | 5–25% APR |
| Merchant cash advance | 40–200%+ effective APR |
| Invoice factoring | 1–5% per 30 days |

Always compare the APR (annual percentage rate) or effective APR across offers — not just the monthly payment. Lendio's comparison interface shows multiple offer details side-by-side. The [CFPB small-business lending resources](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/small-business-lending/) provide guidance on comparing loan offers and understanding your rights as a small business borrower.

## Who Qualifies

Eligibility varies by product and lender within Lendio's network:

**Minimum baseline to access most products:**
- Time in business: 6 months+
- Annual revenue: $50,000+
- Personal credit score: 550+

**For SBA loans specifically:**
- Time in business: 2+ years
- Annual revenue: Varies (strong positive cash flow required)
- Personal credit score: 680+
- Collateral may be required

**For equipment financing:**
- Time in business: 1–2 years (varies by lender)
- Credit score: 620+

Lendio's intake form identifies your situation and routes your application to lenders whose eligibility criteria match your profile.

## Application Process

1. Online application at [lendio.com](https://www.lendio.com/) — approximately 15 minutes
2. Provide business information, revenue, time in business, credit score range
3. Connect bank account or upload bank statements (3 months)
4. Lendio matches your profile to relevant lenders
5. Receive offers from interested lenders (typically within 24–72 hours)
6. Compare offers with help from a Lendio funding manager
7. Accept an offer — complete additional underwriting with the specific lender
8. Funding timeline depends on lender and product (24 hours for MCA; 30–60 days for SBA)

Lendio assigns a funding manager who can explain your options and answer questions. This human touchpoint is valuable for business owners navigating financing for the first time.

## Is Lendio Safe?

Lendio is a legitimate company founded in 2011, headquartered in Lehi, Utah. It has facilitated over $15 billion in small business financing. It is not a lender itself — the risk in the loan relationship is between you and whichever lender extends an offer. Lendio is NMLSID-registered. Before accepting any offer presented through Lendio, verify the lender's credentials independently and read the full loan agreement.
